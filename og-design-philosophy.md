# Chromatic Depth

A visual philosophy for Parallax International Animation Festival.

---

## Philosophy

Chromatic Depth emerges from the tension between infinite digital space and the intimacy of handcrafted motion. It treats color not as decoration but as atmosphere — gradients that breathe like the transition between animation frames, where purple dissolves into magenta and magenta bleeds into ember. Every hue exists on a z-axis, creating the illusion that the composition itself has parallax: foreground elements burn bright while background tones recede into obsidian silence. The palette is meticulously calibrated, each chromatic shift the product of painstaking attention to how light behaves in rendered worlds.

Form manifests through geometric primitives suspended in void — the fundamental building blocks of 3D space reduced to their most elemental state. Spheres, toruses, and stepped volumes float with the weightlessness of objects in a viewport, casting soft shadows that anchor them to an invisible ground plane. These shapes are not random; they are the vocabulary of digital creation, arranged with the deliberate compositional balance of someone who has spent countless hours understanding spatial harmony. Negative space is not emptiness but depth — the darkness between stars, the frames between keyframes.

Typography enters this world as a structural event, not a caption. Letterforms are monumental yet restrained — bold enough to command the composition's architecture, thin enough to let light pass through. Words are sparse: a title, a date, a location. Nothing more. The text does not explain the art; it inhabits it, positioned with the precision of a master typesetter who treats every baseline and kerning pair as a meditation. Scale contrast is dramatic: the festival name dominates while supporting details whisper from calculated positions.

The overall composition must feel like a single frozen frame from an animation that spans galaxies — cinematic in scope, intimate in craft. Every gradient transition, every shadow angle, every spatial relationship between elements should appear as though it was labored over with the care of someone at the absolute top of their field. The result is not a poster but an artifact: proof that the space between dimensions can be mapped, colored, and understood through patient, expert observation.
